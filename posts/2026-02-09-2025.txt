a year to remember... for sure. <3

i know i'm late again with the new year's post. i don't know how i wrote so much so often years ago, but i'm glad i did. and i need to do it more. it always ends up being a release, whether it's through the words themselves or the tears that inevitably flow when i'm writing. like now.

i have a teenager now--what is life?

the twins turn 9 soon--what is life?

i miss my little babies so much, but i adore my 3 besties.

i am constantly overwhelmed with gratitude for all the love in my life, and simultaneously heartbroken across states or homes or both, depending on what week it is. this week the girls are at their dad's, and i fly to denver tomorrow for a conference with eric. next week i'll be back home with my girls, and eric will be back at his place. the week after that, the girls will be back at their dad's, and eric and i will be heading somewhere else.

i love the quality time i get with all of them, but man, it rips my heart into pieces... all of the time. it is quite literally the hardest and most rewarding experience of my life.

how lucky i am to have these people in my life... where i miss them so much that it hurts.

it's always the hardest hit for me the day they leave my house.

ella started taekwondo last year, and got her yellow stripe last month. zoey started jazz classes and has a recital in june. ella is still doing basketball and is loving it. caylin is still playing the flute and had a spring and winter concert.

eric and i went to the netherlands, poland, and sweden. we taught our classes a few times over the course of the year, and gave a talk at a security conference in gothenburg.

we took all 5 kids on a 10-day trip to new england. we started out in maine near the coast, and went to my family reunion. we ate our weight in lobster, and played outside for most of the trip. then we took them all to new york city for a night. then we drove to syracuse to visit my mom and grandma. we got an airbnb on the coast in both places, and it was perfect.

this trip would be the last time the girls and i saw my grandmother. she was on fire that day. she passed away at 94 not long after we visited, and now she and my grandpa are together again. grieving her death resurfaced my other grandparents' deaths for me as well, and it all hit me like a freight train. and then i'm right back to being overwhelmed with gratitude for simply having them as long as we did. my girls got to spend quality time with some of the greatest humans i know.

as far as everything else last year...

storm is still a shitten.

we festival'ed a LOT.

we camped. we glamped.

we went to dollywood.

we went to carowinds.

my mom moved back to north carolina and it's amazing having her just a couple minutes down the road.

i 3d printed and crafted a lot.

the girls are all doing great in school.

the twins ran their first 5K and crushed it.

we have so many girls' nights it's ridiculous, and i wouldn't have it any other way.

they're all obsessed with buldak ramen, skin care, nike socks, and roblox.

it's still chaos, almost always, but despite my exhaustion and the emotional rollercoaster of life, i love every second of it to pieces.

i am so proud of my girls and the good humans they've become. i'm proud of the life we live and how much fun we have. the pictures speak for themselves. <3
