if you don’t know what Web 2.0 is, or if you do know but didn’t realize just how ridiculously huge it has gotten, just go to [www.go2web20.net](http://go2web20.net) and you will quickly learn more than you ever wanted. o\_0 and if you’re anything like me, you will spend the next 7 hours creating accounts on 89% of those websites listed.

i think the only ones i left out were ones i already had, ones that are for kids, or ones for the workplace. since i don’t have one. yet.

i will list my favorites and narrow it down a bit if you are overwhelmed by the extreme SHINY effect of that website. :full: i was like a little kid in a candy store when i went there and i keep going back and finding new things to play with.

TOP 10 APPS:

1. [StoryTlr](http://storytlr.com) – takes all of your social networking RSS feeds and puts them into a lifestream.
2. [TwTip](http://twtip.com) – compilation of twitter’s “tip of the day” tweets.
3. [Plinky](http://plinky.com) – a whole new implementation of question and answer.
4. [Fatburgr](http://fatburgr.com) – let’s just say, i was really surprised when jimmy john’s seemed more unhealthy than mcdonald’s.
5. [Bubble Comment](http://bubblecomment.com) – add video to your twitter.
6. [Jobirn](http://jobirn.com) – lots of employers, lots of job openings, real time.
7. [SmallWorlds](http://smallworlds.com) – you create your own avatar, your own home, dress up and go to the bar with other e-people… it’s cute and kills time. a lot of time.
8. [Bestuff](http://bestuff.com) – what do you think is the best stuff?
9. [FoodFeed](http://foodfeed.us) – keep track of what you eat. i think writing it down for the public to see might make you more conscious of your eating habits
10. [Aviary](http://aviary.com) – amazing designs created on this website alone. it’s incredible. i just wish i could get good at it.

there are hundreds of applications on this site but those are the ones that i’ve been hooked on for the majority of my day. :lookdown: and i wish our guest speaker at our AITP meeting last night had never showed it to me.
